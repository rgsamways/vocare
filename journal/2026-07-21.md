## Feature brainstorm: from "declare your target role" to a full anchor system

Robin asked two things that turned into a lot of design work:
1. Should users be able to declare their intended audience/employer?
2. Could Vocare help surface relevant keywords/phrases and show progress toward matching that audience?

First pass: a `target_role`/`target_industry` field on `users`, fed into M2's conversation prompt and M4's mining pass. Then Robin asked whether a full job description/company context should nudge the live conversation or work more as an **anchor** for reflection. Talked through the tension directly: weighting M2's live follow-up logic against a pasted JD risked recreating the exact scripted-checklist dynamic Vocare exists to reject (the same failure mode as the AI interview that started this project). Landed on: light role/industry fields can steer live tone; richer JD/company content is **anchor-only** — read only by M4's post-session mining, never by M2's live conversation.

This got asked to expand further ("is there a niche/gap/moat we're missing?"). That's where the flat field became a proper **`anchors`** table: multiple anchors per user, each with a dated **revision log** (`anchor_revisions`) rather than being silently overwritten — directly borrowing the shape of Sreditor's own `anchor.md` + `reflect` pattern. Also surfaced (and explicitly parked, not built): reframing the product as "career conversations" rather than "mock interviews," and cross-session narrative callback.

Two real gaps got caught while building this out: **M6's "session history view" was underspecified** (didn't actually say a user could reread a past transcript, just implied a trend line), and **no module had ever specified a profile/account screen at all**. Fixed both — M6 got the real session browser, M1 got a minimal account screen, with anchor management deliberately living in M6 (editing a goal is a progress action, not a settings one).

## Interview-coaching signals: what else is well-established that we're missing

Asked directly: what other standard interview-coaching techniques does the plan not cover yet? Went through several:
- **Outcome/impact and quantified-impact** (STAR's "Result" step) — added as real mining signals.
- **Filler words** ("um"/"uh") — added, but flagged a real technical dependency first: many speech-to-text engines strip disfluencies for readability, so M3 needs to verify the actual STT output preserves them before this feature can work at all.
- **Profanity detection** — considered and explicitly rejected. It's mechanically the same shape as the syntax-trivia scoring this project exists to reject, just applied to different words, and risks undermining the psychological safety that makes people comfortable practicing openly.
- **Progress charts/graphs** — considered and explicitly rejected for now. M6 is 100% text by design; even a chart with no numbers on the axis risks functioning as a disguised score, which is exactly what the no-score framing exists to avoid. Parked, not ruled out forever.

## Crisis-safety: a real correction under real pushback

The spec already had a crisis-safety gap-find (borrowed from Monkeyback's PHQ-9 pattern). Proposed making it more robust — but got real, warranted pushback: *"did you piggyback Monkeyback ideas in? should someone prepping for career goals be scrutinized as crisis?"*

This was the right challenge. Verified where the Monkeyback connection actually lived (it was in the original document, not invented this session) but owned that the specific framing I'd just proposed (a "per-turn classification pass") had drifted toward a clinical-screening tone that doesn't fit Vocare. Recalibrated explicitly: **narrow, almost-always-invisible** — ordinary career stress should never trigger anything; this is a rare-case net for explicit self-harm/acute-crisis language only.

Separately, a real technical bug got caught during a drift audit: the spec described the safety check as shareable with M4's async mining infrastructure — but M4 is explicitly post-session, meaning a crisis check on that timing would only help *after* someone had already logged off. Fixed: decoupled, live, per-turn, in M2 itself. Later added a `country` field (for routing to the correct regional crisis resource — 988 US, Talk Suicide Canada, Samaritans UK — instead of one generic default) and, near the very end of the session, added an explicit exemption so a crisis-triggered session never costs someone one of their three free tries or counts against the paid fair-use cap.

## The brand/logo saga

Robin asked for wordmark concepts, "lean on font, elegant." Built four typography-led directions (Fraunces italic, Cormorant Garamond caps, an upright-Fraunces-plus-mark combo, an app-icon check) on a separate mockup page. Robin picked Concept A and asked to combine it with Concept C's minimal double-arc mark, bolder, set to the left.

What followed was a real lesson in getting small visual details exactly right rather than approximately right, across several rounds:
- The mark was floating above the wordmark's actual baseline — root cause: `align-items: center` centers by full box height, and "vocare" has no descenders, so the invisible descender space in the line-height was throwing off the optical center. Fixed by switching to `align-items: baseline`.
- Asked to fix rounded end-caps that looked clipped. First attempt changed the curve's underlying bezier control points — which, without meaning to, changed the whole silhouette from a peaked mountain shape to a smooth dome. Robin caught this immediately and was right to be frustrated (*"why did you change the design, i just wanted rounded ends"*) — reverted exactly to the prior version rather than continuing to theorize.
- Root cause turned out to be a genuinely small thing: a degenerate bezier control point (the first control point sitting exactly on the path's start point) gave the curve an ambiguous tangent that a round linecap couldn't resolve cleanly. Fixed properly this time by overlaying small filled circles at the endpoints instead of touching the curve — but the first attempt at *that* fix also (wrongly) touched the inner arc, which Robin had explicitly not asked for (*"did i tell you to touch the inner arc? no, i didn't"*) — reverted that part immediately.
- Final real bug, and the most satisfying catch: the filled circles were being **clipped by the SVG's own `viewBox`** — the circle's radius pushed it slightly past the viewBox's bottom edge, and SVG clips at that boundary by default. Fixed by giving the viewBox a little more room. Robin had guessed this exact cause before I'd confirmed it.
- Applied the exact same tuned values (not re-approximated ones) into the actual PDF cover page later, after first getting the scaling proportions wrong there too (mismatched aspect ratio, an independently-guessed gap instead of a scaled one) — fixed by scaling every dimension by the same factor from the known-good header values.
